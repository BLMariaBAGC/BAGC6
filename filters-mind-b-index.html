Filters of the Mind

Stepping into someone else's shoes to understand their map of the world

BAGC6 Executive Reset  ·  Beyond Limits
This exercise is not about you. It is about someone else. The RAS (Reticular Activating System) means that every person experiences the same event through a completely different filter, shaped by their upbringing, past experiences, environment, values, and beliefs. When you genuinely step into another person's shoes and map out how they thought, felt, and acted, you stop judging and start understanding. Your answers are never stored or sent anywhere. When you finish, download a private PDF copy for your own records.
1
The Person and the Situation

Choose a real person whose behaviour, reaction, or decision you have found difficult to understand or accept. From this point, you are mapping their experience, not yours.

REMEMBERFrom this point forward, everything you write is about them. Set your own perspective aside as much as you can.
WHO IS THIS PERSON? Name or describe who they are and your relationship with them.
THE SITUATION Describe what happened. Stick to observable facts only: what was said or done, not what you felt about it.
2
Their Map of the World

Every person's RAS filters reality through their own map, built from their upbringing, past experiences, culture, pressures, and beliefs. The behaviour that confuses you makes complete sense within their map.

1What do you know about their background, upbringing, or past experiences that shaped how they see the world?
2What pressures, fears, or responsibilities are they currently carrying? What does their day-to-day reality feel like from the inside?
3What values or beliefs are most important to them? What does their RAS filter for and protect above everything else?
3
What They Were Thinking

Step fully into their shoes. Based on their map, what story did their brain create about what was happening? You are not judging whether it was correct. You are understanding how it felt true to them.

1Through their RAS filter, what meaning did they attach to this situation? What did their brain tell them was happening?
2What were they trying to achieve, protect, communicate, or avoid, even if it did not come across that way?
3What information did their RAS focus on in this situation? And what might it have dismissed or overlooked entirely?
4
What They Were Feeling

Behaviour is always preceded by feeling. When you can identify what someone was feeling, not what they should have felt but what they actually felt given their map, judgement becomes understanding.

1Given their map of the world, what emotions were most likely present for them in this situation? Consider fear, pressure, shame, pride, frustration, loyalty, or overwhelm.
2Nobody makes mistakes on purpose. Everyone did their best with the tools they had at the time. Given how they were feeling and what their RAS was telling them, does their behaviour now make sense, even if you still disagree with it?
3What do you now understand about this person that you did not fully see before working through their map, thoughts, and feelings?
5
Their Sequence at a Glance

Bring their whole sequence together. This is their map made visible.

Their Filter
Their Thought
Their Feeling and Action
6
From Understanding to Action

Now that you understand their map, something in you can shift. This step is not about fixing them. It is about choosing how you engage differently, having seen things from inside their world.

What I now understand about them
What I am willing to let go of
How I will engage with them differently

Everyone is doing their best with the map they have.

When you understand their map, judgement becomes compassion.
