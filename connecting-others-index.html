Connecting to Others

Identifying the people in your corner and knowing how to reach them

BAGC6 Executive Reset  ·  Beyond Limits

Connection with others is not a luxury. When we connect with people we trust, the brain releases oxytocin, which directly reduces activity in the amygdala, the part of the brain responsible for fear and threat response. This exercise helps you identify who those people are, what kind of support they offer, and how to make better use of the connections you already have. Your answers are never stored or sent anywhere.

Start with a Situation

Before identifying your support network, bring a specific situation to mind. This gives the exercise real anchoring. It works better when it is connected to something current rather than completed.

2
The Four Types of Support

Not all support looks the same, and not everyone in your life offers every type. Name one or two people for each card below.

Before you start
  • The same person can appear in more than one category.
  • If a category feels empty, that is useful information, not a problem to fix immediately.
  • You are mapping what already exists, not what you think you should have.
Emotional Support
Someone you can speak to honestly about what you are going through. You feel safe being yourself with them. They listen without rushing to fix things, and you do not need to perform or manage how you come across.
Who offers me this? Name one or two people.
Informational Support
Someone who can help you think through a problem, offer useful perspective, or point you towards resources and knowledge you do not have. You trust their judgment, and their suggestions help you avoid mistakes you might not have seen coming.
Who offers me this? Name one or two people.
Instrumental Support
Someone who helps in practical terms. This might be assistance with tasks, access to resources, or doing something alongside you that you find difficult to do alone. Their contribution is concrete and tangible.
Who offers me this? Name one or two people.
Companionship Support
Someone whose company restores you. You share interests, or simply enjoy spending time together. They give you a sense of belonging. Being with them requires no effort and leaves you feeling better than before.
Who offers me this? Name one or two people.
