Control Your Thoughts

A guided practice in present-moment awareness

Introduction

Where Are Your Thoughts Right Now?

Sometimes we get so caught up in past experiences that it becomes difficult to experience the present moment fully. Other times, we may worry about a future that has not yet happened and may never happen as we imagine. When we dwell on the past or the future, we miss out on what is happening right now.

Thoughts are simply reactions in the synapses of our minds. They are not concrete truths about the world. And here is the most important thing to understand: a slight interruption in the thought process is enough to take control and decide to have a break from old, repetitive thoughts.

You do not have to stop the thoughts. You only need to notice them, and then return.

The Past

The past is over. It exists only in our memories, not in reality. You cannot change it, relive it, or inhabit it.

You cannot have yesterday's cup of tea right now.

The Present

The present moment is all we truly have. It is where life actually happens, where we can experience, feel, and engage.

This cup of tea. Right now. This one.

The Future

The future has not happened yet and may never happen as we imagine. Worry about it takes you out of what is real.

You cannot have tomorrow's cup of tea today.

This exercise will guide you through a short grounding practice. You can repeat it any time your thoughts pull you away from the present. Take as much time as you need at each step.

Step 1 of 6
Get Comfortable

Read this page fully first. Then close your eyes and carry out the instruction before pressing continue.

Find a comfortable position wherever you are right now. You can sit, lie down, or stand. Allow your body to settle. Unclench your jaw. Drop your shoulders. Let your hands rest somewhere natural.

There is nothing you need to do, achieve, or fix in this moment. You are simply going to be here. When you feel settled, open your eyes, and press continue.

💨
Step 2 of 6
Breathe and Say the Words

Read this page first. Then close your eyes, say the words silently in your mind, and breathe.

Say this silently in your mind:

"The past does not exist. The future has not yet come. The only thing that is real is this moment."

Close your eyes and breathe deeply

With your eyes closed, take three or four slow breaths. Count silently as you go: breathe in for four counts, hold for two, breathe out slowly for six. Notice the coolness of the air as it enters through your nose and the gentle warmth as it leaves. Feel the temperature of the room around you.

If you prefer to keep your eyes open and follow a visual guide, use the breathing circle below.

Step 3 of 6
Ground Yourself

Read through all three prompts below. Then close your eyes and work through each one in turn, taking as much time as you need before opening your eyes and continuing.

👁 Notice Your Feet

Feel your feet on the floor. Notice the pressure, the temperature, the connection to the ground beneath you. If you are sitting, feel the weight of your body through your feet. If you are lying down, notice the soles of your feet and what they are resting against.

✋ Observe Your Hands

Become aware of where your hands are and what they are touching right now. Feel the texture of the surface beneath them. Notice the temperature. Are your hands warm or cool? Heavy or light?

🧒 Feel Your Body

Notice how your body is resting. Feel the support beneath you and the weight of your body settling into it. Scan slowly from the top of your head down to your feet, noticing each part without judgement.

✨
Step 4 of 6
Engage Your Senses

Read through each sense below. Then close your eyes and move through them one by one, staying with each as long as it holds your attention.

🔈

Listen

Notice the sounds around you, near and far. Let them come to you without needing to name or analyse them. Simply hear.

🌡️

Feel Temperature

Notice the temperature of the room on your skin. Is the air warm or cool? Notice the coolness of the air as you breathe in, and the gentle warmth as you breathe out. Feel where the temperature is most present on your face, your hands, your arms.

🌹

Smell

Take note of any scents you can detect. Do not search for them. Simply notice what is already there.

Stay with each sense for as long as you need. There is no correct answer to what you notice. Whatever is there is exactly right.

👁️
Step 5 of 6
Observe Your Surroundings

Your eyes are open. Let your gaze rest softly on the space around you. You are not looking for anything in particular. You are simply arriving back into the room.

See What Is Here

Notice your hands. Your feet. The light in the room. The colours around you. Immerse yourself fully in this present moment.

Pay attention to the visual details of your environment. See the shapes, the shadows, the way light falls on different surfaces. Notice the edges of things. The spaces between things. Let yourself be fully present with what you can see right now.

You are not looking for anything. You are simply seeing. That is enough.

The Now can be reduced to even the second you are breathing in and out. This is where true presence lives. You are here. You are in it right now.

Inner Peace

If you are honest and truly present in this moment, you may feel a quiet sense of stillness. The past and future problems do not exist right now. In this exact moment, you can appreciate the beauty and simplicity of what is happening.

Step 6 of 6
Reflect on What You Noticed

Before you return to your day, take a moment to write down what you noticed. This is not a test. There are no right or wrong observations. Writing it anchors the experience.

What did you notice in your body?

Any sensations, areas of tension, warmth, lightness, or heaviness that you became aware of.

What thoughts were pulling at you before you started?

Past concerns, future worries, or repetitive thoughts that were present when you began.

How do you feel now compared to when you started?

Even a small shift is worth noticing.

You Came Back to the Present

That is all this practice ever asks of you. Not perfection. Not silence. Just a return.

The past does not exist right now

Focus on the only moment that truly exists: this one. Right here. Right now.

Your senses anchor you in the present

Physical sensations are always happening in the present moment. They are your most reliable route back.

A slight interruption is all you need

You do not need to eliminate the thoughts. You only need to notice them and choose, in that moment, to return.

You can do this any time

This practice takes less than five minutes. You can return to it at your desk, in a meeting, on a train, or before sleep.

The Power of Now

The Now can be reduced to even the second you are breathing in and out. That second belongs entirely to you. No past can reach it. No future can disturb it.

A slight interruption in the thought process is enough to take control and break free from repetitive thinking.
