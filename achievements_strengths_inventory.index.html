Achievements and Strengths Inventory

Recognising how your successes and personal qualities intersect

Week 2: Identity Awareness

Why This Exercise Matters

Throughout your professional journey it is crucial to pause and reflect on your path. It is natural to encounter moments of self-doubt, but it is equally important to recognise your abilities and strengths. Your past successes should not fade into the background.

This exercise asks you to recall a minimum of 10 to 15 accomplishments and identify the character strengths, skills, and qualities you used to achieve each one. You will notice that the same personal qualities appear again and again. That pattern is the evidence of who you actually are.

A Helpful Perspective Shift

If you struggle to recall your achievements, picture them as those of a close colleague or friend. Identify the character strengths and skills they would have employed to achieve similar success, then apply that same perspective to your own experiences. This removes the filter of self-minimising.

Your Achievements Inventory
Record each success alongside the strengths that made it possible

For each achievement, describe what you did or accomplished, then identify the specific character strengths, skills, and personal qualities you drew on. Even seemingly minor accomplishments belong here: organising a meeting, completing a project under pressure, navigating a difficult conversation.

Not sure what to write in the strengths column? You will find a full reference guide at the end of this exercise listing character strengths, skills, and personal qualities to prompt your thinking. Scroll down whenever you need it, then come back to continue.

Discovering Your Pattern
The qualities that appear most often are the truest description of who you are

Look back across all fifteen entries. You will notice that certain strengths and qualities appear repeatedly, across very different achievements, in different contexts and different years. That repetition is not coincidence. It is the pattern of who you are.

These are not skills you learned for a particular role. They are qualities you carried into every room you have ever walked into. They are available to you now, in whatever challenge is in front of you.

Which three to five strengths or qualities appear most consistently across your achievements?

Your Evidence for Every Future Challenge

Whenever you find yourself doubting your abilities, or facing a challenge that feels too big, come back to this inventory. Read what you have achieved. Read the strengths and qualities that made each one possible.

Those are not past qualities. They are yours right now. The same resilience, the same judgment, the same character that carried you through everything on this list is available to you in every challenge still ahead. You have already demonstrated who you are. This is the evidence.

Bring this completed inventory to your next session. The pattern you have identified will form the foundation of the identity work we do together, and the affirmations you will build in the next stage.
