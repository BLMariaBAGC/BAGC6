Step 1 of 3

Which pattern created the strongest response when you read it?

The one that tightened something. The one you recognised before you finished the sentence.

Pattern 1
"You know the work is worth more. You cannot bring yourself to say it."
You are preparing your rate or writing a proposal. You know what the number should be. You say a lower one anyway — or hesitate, or soften it before anyone has objected. Afterwards you cannot fully explain why. You are not undercharging because the market will not support more. You are undercharging because something keeps deciding the permission has not been earned yet.
Guilt · Shame · Unworthiness
Pattern 2
"You keep your income quiet. Even when there is nothing to hide."
You underplay what you earn. You deflect questions about your rate. You make yourself financially smaller in rooms where others earn more. Not because you are ashamed of the work — but because something in you is uneasy about what changes when people start to see you differently. The ceiling is not a financial limit. It is a social one.
Fear of rejection · Identity conflict · Belonging
Pattern 3
"A good month feels like a countdown."
The income arrives. And instead of relief there is a low hum of tension. A sense of waiting. As if financial gain carries an expiry date. You are not financially cautious — you are financially vigilant. Constantly scanning for the loss that must be coming. This pattern has kept you exactly comfortable enough. Never beyond it.
Anxiety · Hypervigilance · Dread
Pattern 4
"You see someone at the level you want. Your first thought is: yes, but they're different."
Not different in skill — you have done the comparison. Different in some other way that is hard to name but places them in a category you do not belong to. Given your background. Given where you started. You stay productive, which means you rarely have to sit with the question directly. The ceiling was not built from evidence. It was installed before you had any.
Resignation · Learned helplessness · Quiet grief
Step 2 of 3

Where is this pattern costing you?

Your money story does not stay in your finances. Select every area where you can feel its impact. Choose as many as are true.

Regulation
Health & Nervous System
Financial stress has become a background setting. It affects your sleep, your body, your ability to rest and recover. You carry it physically.
Direction
Vision & Goals
There are professional moves you have not made and a version of your future you have not fully committed to — because financial uncertainty has been part of the calculation.
Connection
Relationships
Money is a source of tension, avoidance, or silence in at least one relationship that matters to you. The conversation that has not happened is affecting the connection that has.
Communication
How You Speak About Your Worth
In professional conversations about your rate, your value, your terms — you have said less than you know, agreed to less than you deserve, or left before the real number was named.
Identity
Who You Believe You Are
Your sense of who you are — professionally and personally — is shaped by what you believe you are allowed to earn, charge, or want. The ceiling is not financial. It is about permission.
Prosperity
Direct Financial Cost
Your current earnings do not fully reflect what your experience, skill, and contribution warrant. There is a gap between what you produce and what you allow yourself to receive.
