Taking Control of Your Emotions

Manage your emotional responses through mindful awareness and visualisation

Introduction

Your emotions move. You can move them.

Emotions are not fixed states. They have direction, momentum, and intensity. When you learn to work with that movement rather than against it, you shift from being a passenger in your emotional experience to being the one in the driver's seat.

This exercise introduces the Spinning Emotion technique. It works by engaging your imagination directly, because whatever you vividly imagine, your nervous system responds to as real.

You are not trying to suppress or ignore the emotion. You are learning to redirect its energy.

The technique has four steps: Notice the emotion and where it lives in your body. Observe how it moves. Reverse its direction with calming colour. Slow it until you reach a place of ease.

Step 2 of 4 -- Observe
Watch It Spin

Close your eyes for a moment and bring the emotion to mind. Imagine it as a spinning sensation inside you. Emotions can spin in any plane: rotating like a clock face, or moving like a wheel entering and leaving the body. Select whichever direction feels instinctively true. There is no right or wrong answer.

↻ Clockwise Rotating to the right
↺ Anticlockwise Rotating to the left
⇓ Forwards Entering the body
⇑ Backwards Leaving the body

Trust whatever direction comes to mind first. Your intuition is correct. The subconscious does not overthink. Neither should you.

Step 3 of 4 -- Reverse

Guidance
When to Use This Technique

The Spinning Emotion technique is most useful when an emotion feels like it has taken hold. These are some of the most common situations where it produces rapid results.

💼
Workplace Pressure
Before important meetings, presentations, or when feeling overwhelmed by competing demands and deadlines.
💬
Difficult Conversations
When emotions run high during challenging discussions and you need to stay grounded and clear-headed.
🔒
Anxiety Moments
During anxious thoughts, spiralling self-doubt, or when the body is holding tension you cannot immediately explain.
🔥
Anger and Frustration
When you feel a reactive emotion building and need to create space between the trigger and your response.
Four Tips for Getting the Most from This
1

Practise when calm

Like any skill, this technique becomes faster and more reliable with repetition. Use it when you are not in distress to build the neural pathway.

2

Trust your first instinct

Whatever direction or colour comes to mind immediately is the right one for you. Do not analyse or second-guess what emerges.

3

Allow time

Some emotions take longer to shift than others. That is completely normal. Move through the stages at whatever pace your nervous system needs.

4

Make it yours

Blue arrows are a suggestion, not a rule. Choose whatever colour and imagery feels most calming to you personally. Your imagination is the tool.
