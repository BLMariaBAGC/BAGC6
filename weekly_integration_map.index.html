Introduction
Welcome

Your goals need fuel.

Most people pursue their goals and neglect their needs. This exercise helps you do both, deliberately, every week.

How this works

In your Define Your Dreams exercise, you decided how you want your life to be. Those are your goals and they do not change week to week. They are your horizon.

Each week, you decide: how will I move toward those goals and feel good while doing it? Because a goal pursued at the expense of your human needs is not sustainable. And a comfortable life that never moves is not fulfilling either.

The sweet spot is forward motion and genuine wellbeing at the same time.

Each week you will map: how you will meet all six human needs, which life areas you are focusing on, what SMART actions you will take, and which behaviours you need to adopt or shift to make it work.

Step 5 of 6

Behaviour to Adopt and Shift

Your actions only work if the behaviours that surround them support them. What do you need to step into? What do you need to notice and move away from?

Class 1 or 2

I am stepping into…

Feels good or uncomfortable but is good for you, good for others, and good long-term.

Class 3 or 4

I am noticing and shifting…

Feels good or bad but is not good for you, not good for others, and not good long-term.

Remember: Class 3 behaviours feel good in the moment but cost you later. Class 4 behaviours feel bad and cost you. Noticing them without judgement is the first step. Choosing differently is the practice.

Step 6 of 6

Your Weekly Review

Return here at the end of the week. Honest reflection is how you improve the week after this one.

The purpose of this review is not to judge yourself. It is to notice patterns, celebrate movement, and make one or two adjustments going forward.

What moved forward this week?

Which actions happened? Which goals advanced? What are you proud of?

Where did the plan not hold?

What got in the way? Was it external or a pattern you recognised? No judgement, just clarity.

How well did you live your behaviour commitment?

Did you step into Class 1 or 2? Where did the old pattern show up?
