Introduction

Why the RAS Hides Who You Are

When self-doubt takes hold, your Reticular Activating System begins filtering reality to match the negative story you are telling about yourself. It screens out your achievements, your capabilities, and evidence of who you actually are, and amplifies everything that confirms the doubt. Over time, your real self becomes harder to access, not because it has gone, but because the brain has stopped flagging it as relevant.

The identity exercises this week exist to reverse that process, to bring your real self back to the surface and make it visible again. Your achievements, your strengths, and your values have not disappeared. They have simply been filtered out.

This archetype exercise adds a further layer. It shows you the role your psyche has been defaulting to, often as a protective response, so you can distinguish between who you have been performing under pressure, and who you actually are.

The questionnaire has 12 questions across three sections: how you operate at work, how you show up in life and relationships, and how you behave under sustained pressure. Answer based on how you actually behave, not how you would like to behave. The most useful answer is the most honest one.

The combination of all three sections gives you a result grounded in the whole person, not just the professional role. Your dominant archetype and your stress pattern may be different from each other. Both are worth knowing.
