What Is Your Money Story?

A Pattern Diagnostic for Professionals

Your relationship with money is not random. It is the product of specific beliefs your brain encoded long before you had the professional life you have today.

This exercise will help you identify which pattern is running your financial decisions right now.

Read each question carefully. Choose the answer that reflects what you actually do, not what you know you should do. Your first instinct is the most accurate.

Question 1 of 5
Question 1 of 5
When you are about to discuss your rates or fees with a potential client, what happens first?
A
You feel a pull to lower the number before they have said anything.
B
You wonder whether they will judge you or think differently of you once they know.
C
A low-level anxiety arrives, a sense that something could go wrong.
D
You feel resigned. It probably will not work out anyway.
Question 2 of 5
You receive an unexpected financial windfall. A bonus, a large payment, an inheritance. What is your instinctive response?
A
Guilt. A sense that you do not quite deserve it or that it should go to someone else.
B
Discomfort about what others will think if they find out.
C
An urge to spend it quickly or a fear that it will disappear before you can use it safely.
D
Mild surprise. These things do not usually happen for people like you.
Question 3 of 5
Someone asks you directly what you earn. How do you respond?
A
You minimise or deflect. The real number feels too exposed to say out loud.
B
You give a vague answer. You do not want to change how they see you.
C
You feel briefly anxious, as though sharing it creates some kind of risk.
D
You share it but immediately feel the number is not impressive enough.
Question 4 of 5
You have an opportunity to charge significantly more for your work. The market supports it. You know it is justified. What stops you?
A
A quiet voice that says you have not quite earned the right to charge that yet.
B
A worry about how you will be perceived. Whether people will think you have changed.
C
A concern that charging more will create problems, expectations, or exposure you are not ready for.
D
A sense that it probably will not work. That people like you do not quite get to operate at that level.
Question 5 of 5
When you look at your financial life honestly, which of these feels most true?
A
I am good at earning but I struggle to feel I deserve what I have built.
B
I keep my financial wins quiet. Success feels safer when it is not too visible.
C
Something always seems to happen that disrupts financial momentum just when things are going well.
D
I have accepted a financial ceiling that I am not sure I actually chose.
